a lazy person's bullet journal

Ever wanted to have a place to put your toughts, plans and creativity in? Are you a lazy person who still wants to feel like they are productive? Have you tried mentaining a journal before but gave up on it after a week? I am here to put your worries to rest and save the day. Here is my guide to a lazy person's journaling. 

Nothing is perfect

Let get this straight. If you expect every page to be exactly as you would imagine it, then this is not the right hobby for you. Most pages look awful, to be fair. However, there are a few that will make you smile every time you see them, and that's what we are aiming for. Just like a photographer choosing 4-5 photos out of 500, we are going to focus on being practical when needed, and artistic when feeling like it. Still, the strategy and the utensils we have will definitely help us to achieve the best result given every messy situation.

Strategy

The most important thing to help you keep up with your project is a proper strategy/structure.

Whenever I started a project like this before, I would drop it not long after because I did not have the right framework in which to do everything consistently. This strategy is based on the original Bullet Journal Theory.

But, firstly, you might want to define what each symbol does so that you always know what your ideas mean. 

An example of a Future Log

Future log

 Every year should start with a Future Log. Here you add the next 6 months and write in general what you have to do in each month. This does not have to be very complex, and you can even write these after a month has passed or throughout each month.

A possible monthly log, with all the days and the highlights for each day.

Monthly log

Then, you have a monthly log in which you have a tab with each day and write the important things from that month. Here you can add "art" or just important notes that you need to notice. You can also have monthly trackers (like going to the gym or "cheat days").

Normally, the page on the left would start with Monday, but here I made an exception since it was the beginning of a month.

Daily log

In the end, you have a daily log. You write each date and the things that you have to do for that day, or the things you already did. This is the most open to change, as the amount you feel like writing could differ from a day to the other. I tend to have 2 pages for every week, but there were times when I had 2 pages for a single day. Having the freedom to change things up is very important.

Advice for beginners

Ideally, you wouldn't miss more than 1-2 days of writing in your journal. However, this is the LAZY person's guide. So here is what to do if you miss even a week of writing and want to catch up:
Go through your phone's photo album and conversations with your friends for each day. Write what you can remember from these resources with "--" and call it a day. If nothing else, write a short summary of the most important parts.

Example of days missed & refilled

Your self expression

This notebook needs to be your safe space, so make sure you always write whatever goes through your mind (or pencil), as well as any notes you might want to carry with you. As I mentioned before, the journal will not look great on every page, so it's important to get that out of the way by making mistakes & "ugly" notes from class.

My classmates & friends also love doodling in my journal. I always carry a few extra markers with me in case of a spontaneous drawing session.

Just have fun with it!
